WFA4133 Fisheries Science Laboratory 2

Laboratory overview

This lab is 3 exercises designed to simulate what a typical fisheries professional is required to do with sampling data, from data entry to preliminary analysis.

  1. The first exercise is a typical situation where data is collected by sampling a small impoundment for bluegill and largemouth bass.
  2. The second exercise works through a commonly encountered issue, where weight is only taken on a subset of fish due to time constraints.
  3. The third exercise is another typical situation where data is collected to assess the condition of a sport fish population.

For this lab you will be randomly divided into teams that you will remain in for the rest of class and expected to work collaboratively through the class exercises. You may or may not work together to answer the lab question for each exercise. NOTE: Your answers to the questions should be written in your own words. This lab is worth 20 points.

Before you get started

  1. Open Chrome and navigate to your group’s web page
  2. Download and unzip the class materials (*.zip) to the desktop of your group’s laptop.
  3. Click on the link the lab web app (can be found on the lab homepage in Chrome)
  4. Follow the steps for exercises 1 – 3 below using the web app. The link is located on the lab homepage.
  5. Your responses to the lab questions must by entered at http://goo.gl/forms/PFdmKVUUXO, by 5 pm the day of the next scheduled lab.

Calculating Size structure indices from length data

  1. On the left hand side, select “largemouth bass” as the species from the filter fish species dropdown.
  2. Enter the appropriate length values from Table 1.1 below to calculate PSD frequencies in the input box on the left hand side. NOTE: Tables are located at the end of this packet.

As a group using whatever tools you have at your disposal, calculate traditional (PSD-X) and incremental (PSD-X-Y) values for Largemouth Bass and Bluegill given the numbers you recorded in Table 1.1 and 1.2 and record them in the corresponding table columns. PSD equations can be found below. NOTE: incremental counts can be calculated from traditional counts by subtracting number of fish in the next largest cell from current cell. For example, if there are 100 stock fish and 20 quality fish, the number of fish that are stock – quality sized is 100-20 = 80. The number of fish in the trophy size class is always the same for traditional or incremental.
  6. Using the counts in Tables 1. 2, calculate the incremental proportion stock density to complete Table 1.2 below. See the below for PSD equations.

TRADITIONAL PSD EQUATION

\(PSD-X=\frac{\text{Number of fish}\ge \text{ specified length}}{\text{Number of fish }\ge \text{ stock length}}\cdot 100\)

INCREMENTAL PSD EQUATION

\(PSD-X-Y=\frac{\text{Number of fish}\,\text{in bin}}{\text{Number of fish }\ge \text{ stock length}}\cdot 100\)
